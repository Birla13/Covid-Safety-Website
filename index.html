What Is Coronavirus?

Coronavirus (COVID-19) is a highly infectious respiratory illness caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. It first emerged in Wuhan, China, in December 2019 and rapidly spread worldwide. Symptoms range from mild to severe, including fever, cough, and difficulty breathing. Vaccines and preventive measures such as mask-wearing and social distancing can help reduce the spread of the virus.

Airborne transmission possible.

Incubation period up to 14 days.

Vaccination helps prevent severe illness.

Why Is It Dangerous?

The danger of Coronavirus lies in its highly contagious nature and the potential for severe respiratory complications. With an airborne transmission possibility, the virus can swiftly spread, leading to widespread outbreaks. The incubation period of up to 14 days adds to the challenge of early detection. Moreover, COVID-19 manifests a spectrum of symptoms, from mild to severe, and in some cases, it can result in critical respiratory issues.

How To Prevent Coronavirus

Practice good hand hygiene by frequently washing hands with soap and water or using hand sanitizer. Wearing masks serves as a protective barrier, reducing the risk of airborne transmission. Social distancing is essential to minimize close contact with infected individuals. Stay informed about vaccination opportunities and consider getting vaccinated to bolster individual and community immunity. Regularly disinfect frequently-touched surfaces, and adhere to local guidelines and restrictions.
